Introduction


Detecting different types of tumors is crucial for early diagnosis and effective treatment. Tumors, which are abnormal growths of tissue, can be either benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). The process of identifying these tumors has been significantly enhanced by advancements in medical imaging, laboratory tests, and genetic screening. Modern techniques such as X-rays, ultrasounds, CT scans, MRI, and PET scans provide detailed images that help in locating and characterizing tumors. Biopsies, where a sample of tissue is examined under a microscope, offer a definitive diagnosis. Additionally, blood tests can detect specific markers that indicate the presence of cancer, and genetic tests can identify mutations linked to a higher risk of developing certain cancers. Early detection is key to successful treatment, making regular screenings and awareness of symptoms essential. As technology continues to advance, our ability to detect tumors earlier and more accurately continues to improve, offering hope for better outcomes in cancer care.
